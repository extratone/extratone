kaniini October 23, 2019, 1:58am #20

I added explicit regression tests in commit 2777aea45b which shows that Pleroma can handle to and cc single-value messages just fine.

As I mentioned, this is related to a spam protection we have on user inboxes. That will be fixed, but asking somebody to use arrays (as it is something I know we have had issues with before) and see if it works does not mean that we do not consider it a bug. I do not appreciate this blowing up into something that it did not need to be.

As for JSON-LD safety in Pleroma, the main area of weakness has to do with the fact that protocol modules are responsible for demarshalling data to Pleroma‚Äôs IR and committing the side effects at the same time, which leads to people occasionally messing up and assuming that their code is running in an IR-safe context when they are actually processing JSON-LD. I could write a very long-winded explanation about what Pleroma IR is, but a simple explanation is that Pleroma IR is a very strict subset of JSON-LD that is optimized for how we store our view of the distributed graph in our database.

The plan is to split side effect committal and IR into two phases. CommonsPub did something similar in their fork, but I don‚Äôt particularly like the approach there. And of course, they moved away from storing the activities as graphs altogether.
