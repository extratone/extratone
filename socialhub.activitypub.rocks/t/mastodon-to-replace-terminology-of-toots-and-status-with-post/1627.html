aschrijver April 20, 2021, 8:20am #1

Just found this out via @trwnh@mastodon.social who pointed to this #software:mastodon Pull Request by Eugen:

github.com/tootsuite/mastodon

Change the nouns "toot" and "status" to "post"

As far as I can tell, "posts" were first changed to "toots" in #4608, and I think that was a mistake. It was originally spurred by a never-ending stream of questions of "what do we call posts here?", but I don't think every platform needs a cutesy branded name for whatever is on it. Videos on YouTube aren't yeets, they are just videos. Posts on facebook are just posts. Twitter is an exception. I was also not originally aware that "toot" was slang for "fart" in some regions. It makes Mastodon harder to take seriously. In this PR, the publish button still says "Toot!" but it's worth thinking about as well.
